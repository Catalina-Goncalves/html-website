Places to Visit

  1. EUROPE

    • Rome, Italy

      • Rome, Italy’s vibrant capital, is a sprawling metropolis of 2.8 million people where millennia of history blend with modern life. Known as the "Eternal City", it served as the center of the Roman Empire and Western civilization, leaving behind a wealth of world-famous ancient ruins, Renaissance art, and global cultural treasures.




    • Transylvania, Romania

      • Transylvania, a historic region in central Romania, is renowned for its dramatic Carpathian mountain landscapes, dense virgin forests, and rich blend of Romanian, Hungarian, and Saxon cultures. Best known globally for the vampire legends of Count Dracula, it offers travelers stunning medieval towns, dramatic fortresses, and pristine, biodiverse nature.




    • Nacional Park, Croatia

      • Croatia is home to eight spectacular national parks, famously recognized for their cascading turquoise waterfalls, lush forests, pristine Adriatic archipelagos, and rich geological diversity. The two most iconic parks are Plitvice Lakes and Krka, both of which draw millions of visitors to their interconnected lake and waterfall systems.





  2. ASIA

    • Tokyo, Japan

      • Tokyo, Japan’s bustling capital, is the world's most populous metropolis and a captivating blend of ultramodern skyscrapers and ancient temples. Located in the Kantō region of Honshu, it serves as the nation's premier hub for economy, culture, and cuisine.




    • Bali, Indonesia

      • Bali is a world-renowned Indonesian island province located in the Lesser Sunda Islands. Famed as the "Island of the Gods", it is the only Hindu-majority island in the country. Bali offers a striking blend of dramatic volcanic peaks, terraced rice paddies, pristine beaches, and a deeply spiritual culture.




    • The Islands, Thailand

      • Thailand’s tropical islands offer world-class beaches, dramatic limestone cliffs, and vibrant marine life. The islands are broadly divided into two main regions: the Andaman Sea (famous for diving and karst scenery) and the Gulf of Thailand (known for island-hopping and party scenes).






  3. CANADA

    • Vancouver

      • Vancouver is a major, bustling coastal seaport in British Columbia, Canada, renowned globally for its stunning natural beauty, where snow-capped mountains meet the Pacific Ocean. Consistently ranking as one of the world's most livable cities, it has a metropolitan population of over 2.6 million and a highly diverse, multicultural community.




    • Niagara Falls

      • Niagara Falls is a world-famous group of three massive waterfalls spanning the international border between Ontario, Canada, and New York, United States. Formed 12,000 years ago by glacial activity, it is celebrated for its immense flow volume and spectacular nighttime illuminations.




    • Gros Morne National Park (Newfoundland)

      • Gros Morne National Park is a breathtaking UNESCO World Heritage Site on Newfoundland’s west coast, famous for its geological diversity Parks Canada. Glacial action created dramatic, landlocked fjords like Western Brook Pond, while colliding continents thrust the Earth's mantle into plain sight at the Tablelands.
